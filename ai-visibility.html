AI tools don't invent answers for search queries about local businesses. Their recommendations are driven by clean, structured, cross-referenced data from sources they already know and trust. The Google Business Profile is the single richest source of this kind of data anywhere on the web.

Here's what makes a GBP genuinely attractive to AI tools — and how Paige optimizes every aspect:

Structured Data & Categories

LLMs feast on clean, schema-defined fields — name, address, hours, categories, services, attributes — and AI engines match queries to business types via those category and service tags. Get the wrong ones, get filtered out. Paige optimizes every structured field and picks the highest-converting category combinations based on what's working across 10,000+ similar profiles.

Verifiable, Cross-Referenced Identity

AI tools weight Google-verified businesses higher and rely on consistent NAP (name, address, phone) data across the web to confirm legitimacy. Mismatches confuse AI engines and tank visibility. Paige keeps the profile current, syncs identical NAP data across 62 citation directories, detects duplicates, and pushes synced updates the moment a client's info changes.

Recency Signals

AI engines reward businesses that look active: daily posts, fresh photos, recent reviews. Paige publishes daily GBP posts, drips geotagged photos, replies to reviews instantly, and pushes daily content to Facebook, Instagram, and LinkedIn.

Indexable Video Content

YouTube is currently the #2 most-cited source in Google AI Overviews, and video transcripts get crawled for local queries. Paige creates and publishes YouTube videos for your clients automatically — no shooting, no editing, no uploading.

Most agencies only do one or two of these — manually, sporadically, and only for the clients who scream loudest. Paige does all four, every day, for every client, on autopilot.

If your clients haven't already asked you what you're doing to help them optimize for AI search results, they're going to. Let Paige take all of that important, but tedious work off your plate so you can let them know you've already taken care of it.
